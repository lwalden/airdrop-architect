## 1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit websites. They help websites remember your preferences and understand how you use the site.

## 2. How We Use Cookies

Airdrop Architect uses cookies on our web dashboard. Our Telegram bot does not use cookies.

### 2.1 Strictly Necessary Cookies

These cookies are essential for the website to function. They cannot be disabled.

| Cookie | Purpose | Duration |
|--------|---------|----------|
| session_id | Maintains your login session | Session |
| csrf_token | Security protection | Session |

**Legal Basis:** Legitimate interest (essential for service operation)

### 2.2 Analytics Cookies

These cookies help us understand how visitors use our website.

| Cookie | Purpose | Duration | Provider |
|--------|---------|----------|----------|
| _ga | Google Analytics - distinguishes users | 2 years | Google |
| _gid | Google Analytics - distinguishes users | 24 hours | Google |

**Legal Basis:** Consent (you can accept or reject these)

### 2.3 Functional Cookies

These cookies remember your preferences.

| Cookie | Purpose | Duration |
|--------|---------|----------|
| theme | Remembers light/dark mode preference | 1 year |
| language | Remembers language preference | 1 year |

**Legal Basis:** Consent

## 3. Your Cookie Choices

### 3.1 Cookie Banner

When you first visit our web dashboard, you will see a cookie banner with equal options to:
- **Accept All Cookies** - Enables all cookie categories
- **Reject All Cookies** - Only strictly necessary cookies are used
- **Manage Preferences** - Choose specific cookie categories

### 3.2 Changing Your Preferences

You can change your cookie preferences at any time by:
- Clicking the "Cookie Settings" link in the website footer
- Clearing cookies in your browser settings

### 3.3 Browser Settings

Most browsers allow you to:
- See what cookies are stored
- Delete individual or all cookies
- Block cookies from specific or all sites
- Block third-party cookies

Note: Blocking all cookies may affect website functionality.

## 4. Third-Party Cookies

## 5. Do Not Track Signals

Our website responds to Do Not Track (DNT) browser signals. If your browser sends a DNT signal, we will not load analytics cookies.
